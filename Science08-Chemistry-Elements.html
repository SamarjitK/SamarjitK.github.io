Atomic Structure

In greek atom means 'cannot be split' because it is so small. It has protons electrons and neutrons.

The protons (positive charge) and neutrons(neutral) are tightly packed in the center called the nucleus.

Electrons, which have almost no mass at all, rapidly orbit around the nucleus.

Between the nucleus and electrons, there is a vast amount of empty space - a vacuum.

A scientist, Ernest Rutherford, fired small particles at gold foil - and 99% of them went through, only 1 in 8000 bouncing back.

This led him to believe that the gold atoms were mostly empty space, with most of the mass in the nucleus in the center.

The proton and neutron both have a mass of 1 AMU (Atomic Mass Unit), while the electron's mass is negligible.

The mass number is therefore the sum of only the number of protons and neutrons.

The atomic number is the number of protons in an element. Proton has positive charge, electron negative.

The atom has no charge, as the number of protons and electrons is equal.

Electronic Structure

Electrons orbit the nucleus in shells or orbits.

In the first orbit, there is room for two electrons, in the second shell, 8 electrons, in the third, 8, and so on.

For example, Pottasium's (19 electrons) electron structure is 2,8,8,1.

Alkali Metals

Group 1 is the Alkali metals, so called because they form an alkali when they react with water.

Down the group: Lithium (Li), Sodium (Na), Potassium (K), Rubidium (Rb), Caesium (Cs), and Francium (Fr).

Their melting and boiling points decrease as you go down the group.

They react more vigorously as you go down. While the first the metals reacting with water are safe, the reaction of Rubidium or Caesium is very explosive

Francium, which is radioactive and has a half-life of only 22 minutes,put in water, is untested, as it would cause a Tsunami and destroy parts of the earth.

Halogens

Halogens are group 7, meaning salt-producing, as in reaction with metal, they form salt.

Fluorine (F), Chlorine (Cl), Bromine (Br), Iodine (I), and Astatine (At). As you go down the group:

Their reactivity decreases, and melting and boiling points increase.

F and Cl are gases, Br liquid, I and At are solid.

Noble Gases

They are all gases, and are inert, meaning that they are unreactive and don't form compounds.

Helium (He), Neon (Ne), Argon (Ar), Krypton (Kr), Xenon (Xe), and Radon (Rn).
