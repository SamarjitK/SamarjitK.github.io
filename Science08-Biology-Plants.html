Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is a process that plants use to make food. Think of it as a recipe:

The ingredients are Carbon Dioxide and Water...and the end products are Glucose and Oxygen.

But as with any recipe, you don't just wait for the Ingredients to become food! You supply energy by turning on the oven, giving the ingredients heat.

The plant absorbs energy from the Sun, and uses it to make Glucose, the plant's food.

CO2 + H2O -> C6H12O6 + O2

...and the balanced equation (see chemistry) is...

6CO2 + 6H2O -> C6H12O6 + 6O2

There's just one problem, though. Glucose is soluble (dissolves) in water. It is a Sugar which is a carbohydrate.

...And you can't store a cake which turns into mush quickly. So, the plant truns the glucose into starch.

Starch is a chain of glucose molecules, and does not dissolve in water.

Starch is stored in the chloroplast of leaf cells, wher photosynthesis takes place.


Mineral Salts: Fertilizers

Fertilizers contain mineral salts. These help the plant grow larger and stronger.

There are to main kinds of fertilizer: Nitrates and Magnesium

Nitrates

Nitrates help the plant make proteins. These proteins help the plant make new cells.

It is also needed to make chlorophyll. Plants short of Nitate will be stunted and yellow.

Magnesium

Like Nitrate, it is needed to make chlorophyll. Magnesium deficiency makes the plant grow yellow, lacking chlorophyll, which is the green pigment.

Plants and Water

When each cell of a plant contains a lot of water, they all press out on each other, keeping the plant structure firm.

Water deficiency make the plant soft and floppy, making it collapse, which is called wilting.

When water travels up the xylem, it also carries dissolved nutrients, such as mineral salts.

Water then evaporates through the leaves, and when it does, it absorbs some of the plant's heat, cooling it down.

Last but absolutely not least, it is a key ingredient in Photosynthesis.

Flower

NOTE: This is an activity. You either need a real flower, or an unlabelled picture of one.

Flowers are brightly colored and scented to attract pollinators, which you will learn about in the next unit.

On the outside, there are the brightly colored petals, and at the base of the flower, green petal-like sepals can be found.

Around the center, there are many structures called Stamens, which have anthers connected to the flower by thin Filaments.

The anthers on the tip of the stamens have grains of pollen, which contains the male gamete.

The center of the flower is the Carpel, made of the Stigma, style, and ovary.

The stigma and style (from top to bottom), are the tip of the carpel and its connection to the ovary (bulb shaped), respectively.

Inside the ovary, the ovule is found, which contains the female gametes.

Pollination

Remember how the Anthers have pollen grains, which contain the male gametes of a plant?

As a refresher, when the male and female gamete meet, they form a seed, which we will explain in the next lesson(fertilization)

But the problem is getting one plant's male gamete to the other plants female gamete.

So, the plants secrete a sugary fluid, called nectar, to attract pollinators, which are insects and birds.

When the pollinator comes to collect nectar, some pollen sticks onto them.

When it goes to another plant, some pollen may rub off onto the other flower's stigma, where the story will be continued in the next lesson.

However, some plants rely on the wind to blow their pollen to other plant, with a chance that it just might land on another plant's stigma.

Fertilization

After pollen falls on the stigma, it grows a tube through the style, into the ovary, and into the ovule.

The male gamete goes down the tube, and then fuses with the female gamete in the ovule, forming a zygote.(meiosis)

The zygote cell divides many times(mitosis), becoming a group of cells called the embryo.

The ovule grows into a seed around the embryo. NOTE: The following is an activity, required a well soaked bean or lima bean seed.

There is a Testa, an outer covering, and the Micropyle, a tiny hole from which the tube from the pollen grain had first entered.

The bulk of the seed are the two cotyledons found from peeling the testa. It is the food stores for when the embryo first germinates.

Pull these apart, and you will find the small embryo plant, which formed from the zygote after fertilization.

Fruits

While the ovule is busy becoming a seed, the overy becomes a fruit.

Fruits help in seed dispersal, which disperses the seed so that it germinates far from the parent plant.

If the seed germinates nearby, it will have to compete for resources with the parent plant, lowering its chance of survival.

There are three types of seed dispersal:

Parachute shaped fruits like dandelions, carry their seeds on the wind.

Hooked or spiky fruits latch on to unknowing animals, which carry away the seeds.

Fleshy fruits like apples and mangos, attract birds, which eat the fruits and carry the seeds on accident.
