Keys

A key is a set of questions that you can use to identify an organism.

The first question set will have an a and b. Here is an example of a key for crab, earthworm, and slug:

1 a has legs ....................crab

1 b does not have legs ..........go to 2

2 a body not made up of rings ...slug

2 b body made up of rings .......earthworm

You can make questions based on color, texture, features, numbers, behavior, and many other criteria.

Variation

Living organisms are classified into species, though no 2 organisms of a species are exactly alike.

They show variation, difference among individuals within a species

This is because of two things: genes and environment.

You have genes in te nucleus of every cell, which decide things like you gender, and hair color.

Your environment also affects your variation, like how people who do heavy lifting will have large wrists.

Inheritance

In the nucleus of each cell there are chromosomes which give you genes. The are made out of DNA.

When you are born, you recieve half of each parents genes, which becomes a zygote cell, which was your first cell.

Genes, which are instructions, come in many different forms.

One form might give you curly hair, another might make it straight.

Genes are inherited from your parents, though some things like your behavior might be altered by your environment.

Selective Breeding

Lets say you have a population of sheep, and you want to get more wool.

You would only choose the sheep with longer wool, because you know that they would pass on their genes of long wool to their offspring.

You would breed these chosen ones together, and the result would be a sheep with longer wool

This is called selective breeding. If you did this for many generations, you would end up with a population of sheep with only longer wool.

Natural Selection

In selective breeding, we humans select the animals with the features we want and allow themm to reproduce.

This also happens in nature, but is a slower process. We already know there is variation within populations.

Let's say that there are bears, but some have thicker fur than others. Over time, the climate becomes colder.

In the colder weather, the bears with thin fur are more likely to die. Similarly, the thick furred bears would have a greater chance of survival.

The thick furred bears would be the only survivors, and reproduce, and their offspring would inherit the thick fur and also survive.

This is natural selection, where nature selects the variations best for their survival.

Charles Darwin was a scientist who laid the foundation for the study of evolution in his book, the origin of species.

In his book, he proposed the theory natural selection:

The individuals that are best adapted to their environment are the ones that are most likely to pass on their features to the next generation.
