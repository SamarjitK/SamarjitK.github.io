Characteristics of living things

For an organism to be considered living, it must have seven characteristics:

  1. Movement

    An action by an organism, or part of one, causing a change of position.

  2. Respiration

    The chemical reactions in cells that break down nutrients and release energy for metabolism.

  3. Sensitivity

    The ability to detect or sense stimuli in either the internal or external environment and to make appropriate responses.

  4. Growth

    A permanent increase of size and dry mass by in increase in cell number and/or size.

  5. Excretion

    The removal from the organism of waste products of metabolism, excess, and toxic, substances.

  6. Nutrition

    Taking in of materials for energy, growth, and development; plants need H2O, CO2, ions and water, and animals need organic compounds, ions, and H2O.

  7. Reproduction

    The processes that make more of the same species.
